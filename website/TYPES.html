Fine Dining:

High-end, formal restaurants that offer elaborate, multi-course meals with top-quality ingredients and attentive service. They often have dress codes and reservations.

Casual Dining:

More relaxed than fine dining, with table service and moderately priced meals. These restaurants may have a specific theme or decor style.

Fast Food:

Quick, budget-friendly meals with limited table service. Menus are often limited and focus on speed, with popular items like burgers, fries, and sandwiches.

Cafes and Bistros:

Often smaller with a more casual environment, serving lighter meals, coffee, pastries, and beverages. Bistros may offer more meal options, especially with European influences.

Buffet:

Self-service dining with a variety of dishes laid out for patrons to choose from, typically at a fixed price.

Ethnic/International:

Focus on cuisine from specific regions or countries, offering diners a taste of different cultures (e.g., Italian, Indian, Chinese).
